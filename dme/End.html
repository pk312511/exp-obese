An easy interpretation to this value

Probability/delay discounting refers to the decrease in the subjective value of a reward as a function of a decreasing likelihood of its receipt. Thus, rewarding outcomes with shorter delays until their receipt and those with a greater likelihood of receipt tend to have a higher subjective value than those associated with longer delays or a lower likelihood.

In this experiment, we calculate it via the exponential approach. Exponential discounting, a cornerstone of behavioral economics, unveils why we often prioritize immediate rewards over future gains. It encapsulates the notion that money's value diminishes over time, making a dollar today more attractive than a dollar tomorrow. This phenomenon holds insights into decision-making dynamics, influencing choices about spending, saving, and more. The model introduces a discount factor that quantifies our inclination toward impulsivity or patience. People with higher discount factors (yours: {{player.discounting}}) lean towards immediate gratification , while those with lower factors exhibit patience . While exponential discounting aids in explaining our trade-offs between now and later, it also sparks discussions about the complexities of human decision-making, underscoring the delicate balance between short-term desires and long-term goals.
