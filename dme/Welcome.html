Welcome to the Decision Making Experiment.

authored by Xiaoyi Guo

Welcome to the decision making experiment. This research is based on Bickel et al. (2020)’s experiment investigating whether obesity could affect the behavior of risk-taking.

Before you officially start, you may want to know basic information of this experiment. This experiment consists of one demographic information collection session and 21 subsessions of behavioral questions. All behavioral questions are multiple choice so that you could pick one out of the three possible alternatives. This experiment may take you 20 to 30 minutes, and as a compensation of your participation, you would be awarded 2.5 € immediately after you complete all of the questions and another 4 € after we examine that your data is eligible for further research needs. We strongly recommend you to read the following details, if you agree so, please click the "start the experiment" button to start experiment.

Your participation in this study is purely voluntary and you will remain anonymous throughout the study. Results may include summary data, but you will never be identified. By continuing, you consent to the publication of study results.

Data Privacy and Prior Consent

You are free to withdraw at any time without giving a reason. The experiment is completely anonymous and the data collection complies with GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation of the European Union).

Link to GDPR »

No Real Loss is Involved

In this experiment, you are introduced to face choices between different arts of payment. All money herementioned are virtual and you will not actually lose or gain things. However, you should situate yourself into such context and provide your authentic feedback.

Individual Choice and No Regret

Neither your choice across questions nor your response will affect the order of questions raising. Please alert that the question positioning method is irrelevant to your answers and do not have a particular order. Additionally, you are not allowed to return to alter your prior answers once you submitted, which means all your decisions are final.
